\chapter{Frequency Response and Bode Plots}
\section{What a Bode Plot Shows}
A Bode plot is a pair of graphs against logarithmic frequency:
\[
20\log_{10}|G(\jj\omega)|,
\qquad
\angle G(\jj\omega).
\]
The magnitude plot shows gain or attenuation. The phase plot shows phase lead or
lag. In radio language, this is how filters, amplifiers, couplers, and matching
networks reveal what they do across frequency.

\begin{controlsbox}
Frequency response is the response of a linear system to sinusoidal inputs. In
state-space form, the same idea can be written as
\[
G(\jj\omega)=C(\jj\omega I-A)^{-1}B+D.
\]
This is a conceptual bridge, not an exam calculation requirement.
\end{controlsbox}

\section{First-Order Low-Pass}
For the RC low-pass output across the capacitor,
\[
G(s)=\frac{1}{1+sRC},
\qquad
G(\jj\omega)=\frac{1}{1+\jj\omega RC}.
\]
\[
|G|=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+(\omega RC)^2}},
\qquad
\angle G=-\tan^{-1}(\omega RC).
\]
The corner frequency is
\[
\omega_c=\frac{1}{RC},
\qquad
f_c=\frac{1}{2\pi RC}.
\]
At \(\omega_c\), the magnitude is \(1/\sqrt{2}\), or approximately
\(-\SI{3}{\decibel}\).

\section{Approximate Rules}
\begin{itemize}
\item One first-order pole contributes approximately \(-\SI{20}{\decibel}\) per
decade after its corner.
\item One first-order zero contributes approximately \(+\SI{20}{\decibel}\) per
decade after its corner.
\item \(\SI{20}{\decibel}\) per decade is approximately \(\SI{6}{\decibel}\) per
octave.
\item A second-order pair changes slope at about \(\SI{40}{\decibel}\) per
decade.
\end{itemize}

\section{Bandwidth, Resonance, and Half-Power Points}
Bandwidth is the useful frequency span of a circuit or system. For a resonant
response, the half-power points are the frequencies where power is half the
reference level. In magnitude terms that is
\[
20\log_{10}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)=-\SI{3.0103}{\decibel}.
\]
High \(Q\) means weak damping, sharper resonance, and narrower bandwidth.

\section{Exam Relevance}
\begin{exambox}
Bode plots themselves may not be drawn on the exam, but the ideas behind them
are everywhere: cutoff frequency, gain, attenuation, phase shift, bandwidth,
filter slope, and resonant peaking.
\end{exambox}
