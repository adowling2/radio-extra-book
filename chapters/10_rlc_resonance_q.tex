\chapter{RLC Resonance, Damping, and Q}
\section{Why Resonance Matters}
Resonance is central to tuned receivers, oscillators, matching networks, traps,
crystal filters, antenna loading, and selective RF circuits. It is where energy
exchange between electric and magnetic fields becomes especially efficient.

\section{Series RLC Governing Equations}
For a series RLC circuit,
\[
LC\frac{\dd^2 v_C}{\dd t^2}
+RC\frac{\dd v_C}{\dd t}
+v_C=u(t).
\]
The undamped natural angular frequency is
\[
\omega_0=\frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}.
\]
For a series circuit, a useful damping parameter is
\[
\alpha=\frac{R}{2L}.
\]
The damped natural frequency, when underdamped, is
\[
\omega_d=\sqrt{\omega_0^2-\alpha^2}.
\]
It is not always equal to \(\omega_0\).

\section{Series Resonance}
The impedance is
\[
Z=R+\jj\left(\omega L-\frac{1}{\omega C}\right).
\]
At resonance,
\[
\omega L=\frac{1}{\omega C},
\qquad
\omega=\omega_0.
\]
The net reactance is zero, the impedance is purely resistive, and current is
maximum for a fixed applied voltage.

\section{Parallel Resonance}
An ideal parallel LC network has admittance
\[
Y=\jj\omega C+\frac{1}{\jj\omega L}
=\jj\left(\omega C-\frac{1}{\omega L}\right).
\]
At resonance, the ideal branch admittances cancel and the input impedance is
large. Real losses keep the impedance finite.

\section{Q and Bandwidth}
Quality factor \(Q\) measures sharpness of resonance. For a series RLC circuit,
\[
Q_s=\frac{\omega_0 L}{R}=\frac{1}{\omega_0RC}.
\]
For a parallel RLC circuit the roles of \(R\) invert---the loss resistance now
appears in parallel---so \(Q\) is resistance \emph{divided by} reactance,
\[
Q_p=\frac{R}{\omega_0 L}=\omega_0 RC,
\]
the reciprocal form of the series expression. For many narrowband resonant
circuits,
\[
BW\approx \frac{f_0}{Q}.
\]

\begin{mistakebox}
Do not present one topology's Q formula as universal. Loaded Q, unloaded Q, and
component Q answer different questions. Loaded Q includes the effect of source
and load coupling; unloaded Q describes the resonator's internal losses;
component Q describes the loss of a particular inductor, capacitor, or other
part.
\end{mistakebox}

\section{Energy Balance}
Using
\[
\mathcal{H}=\frac{1}{2}Cv_C^2+\frac{1}{2}Li^2,
\]
the series RLC energy balance is
\[
\frac{\dd\mathcal{H}}{\dd t}=u(t)i(t)-Ri^2(t).
\]
The inductor and capacitor exchange energy; the resistor dissipates it.

\begin{controlsbox}
High \(Q\) means weak damping and narrow bandwidth. In second-order controls
language, it corresponds to lightly damped modes and a sharper frequency-domain
peak.
\end{controlsbox}

\section{Worked Example}
\enlargethispage{3\baselineskip}
Let \(L=\SI{10}{\micro\henry}\) and \(C=\SI{220}{\pico\farad}\).
\[
f_0=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}
=\frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{(10\times10^{-6})(220\times10^{-12})}}
\approx \SI{3.39}{\mega\hertz}.
\]
If the series resistance is \(\SI{5}{\ohm}\),
\[
Q_s=\frac{\omega_0L}{R}
=\frac{2\pi(3.39\times10^6)(10\times10^{-6})}{5}
\approx 42.6.
\]
