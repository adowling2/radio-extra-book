\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}
This mini-textbook is a polished LaTeX edition of the supplied Word manuscript
\emph{Ham Radio Circuit Theory for the Amateur Extra Exam}. The Word document
remains the authoritative source for the book's content, order, tone, and central
idea: the same circuit can be understood as a physical object, a set of
conservation laws, a differential equation, a state-space system, a phasor
calculation, a frequency response, and an energy balance.

The intended reader is an engineer who already knows conservation laws,
differential equations, eigenvalues, state-space systems, and classical
frequency-response ideas, but wants those tools reconnected to amateur-radio
circuit questions. Laplace transforms and state-space models are used as bridges
to familiar engineering language. They are not presented as calculations the
Amateur Extra exam directly asks the reader to perform.

Reference PDFs in the project were used only as supporting checks for topic
coverage, terminology, formulas, and historical question-pool alignment. Their
prose, questions, answer choices, and figures were not copied.
